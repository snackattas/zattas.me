About Me


Hey, I'm Zach. I'm a full stack developer, healthcare enthusiast, technologist, lifelong learner, chef, type-1 diabetic, and friend. Right now I'm studying web development independenly, patiently waiting to get signed up for my first gig.

I love to experiment. When cooking, I'll always have a new new batch of something fermented on hand. When writing short stories, I always try to come up with premises that are out of the box. When traveling, I always leave part of my carefully-planned itinerary blank (for novel adventures to occur in).

I graduated from Wesleyan University in 2013 with a degree in Philosophy and the Science in Society Program.

When I decided to pursue coding, I enrolled in Udacity's Intro to Programming Nanodegree. Soon after I found myself enrolled in Udacity's Full Stack Web Developer Nanodegree.

However, I'd say only %30 percent of my learning came from those courses. The rest is from a random assortment of MOOCs, textbooks, podcasts, blogposts, code documentation, stack overflow articles, meetup.com events, and coding small projects myself. Self-guided learning is my thing.

And that's my favorite part of web development so far -- since the field's always changing, there's so much to learn. I'm ready to rise to the challenge, leave my comfort zone, and code some stuff!
