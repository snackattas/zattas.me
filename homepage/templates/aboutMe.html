About Me


Hey, I'm Zach. I'm a full stack developer, healthcare enthusiast, technologist, lifelong learner, chef, type-1 diabetic, and friend. Right now I'm studying web development.

I love to experiment. When cooking, I'll always have a new batch of something fermented on hand. When writing short stories, I always come up with out-of-the-box premises. When traveling, I always leave part of my carefully-planned itinerary blank (for novel adventures to occur in).

I graduated from Wesleyan University in 2013 with a degree in Philosophy and the Science in Society Program. I chose Philosophy because I like to ask big questions. I chose the Science in Society Program because I like science, but I wanted tools to question scientific practice. Also, it's a mouthful to say.

At Epic, my first job post-college, I learned​ Caché. With these new skills, I began to write small scripts to make testing easier. Next, I began to test at the API level. Soon after, I was writing unit tests, which very few QAers were writing at that time. A year after starting at Epic, I was no longer intimidated by technical concepts. I was testing heavily technical areas like environment management, batch scheduling, auditing, indexing, etc.

When I decided to pursue programming as a profession, I enrolled in Udacity's Intro to Programming Nanodegree. Soon after I found myself enrolled in Udacity's Full Stack Web Developer Nanodegree.

However, I'd say that only 30% percent of my learning came from those courses. The rest is from a random assortment of MOOCs, textbooks, podcasts, blogposts, code documentation, stack overflow articles, meetup.com events, and coding small projects myself. Self-guided learning is my thing.

And that's my favorite part of web development so far -- since the field's always changing, there's so much to learn. I'm ready to rise to the challenge, leave my comfort zone, and code!
